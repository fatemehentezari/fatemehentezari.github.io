Welcome to my page!

I'm a PhD researcher in Economics at the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology, specializing in Development Economics with a focus on gender issues. My current research focuses on the socio-economic landscapes of Vietnam and Iran. I have access to VHLSS and Vietnamese Enterprise data, and have cleaned these datasets extensively. Feel free to reach out if you need assistance with cleaning these datasets. Beyond academia, I'm a proud mother and a photographer with a passion for capturing the beauty of nature through my lens.

Enjoy exploring my work!

Expertise

My working papers.

My working papers focus on significant economic and leadership topics that impact global development, with a focus on gender issues.

Women Leadership

Trade Liberalization and Female Leadership in Vietnamese Firms

We are examining the improved performance of female-led firms, factories, and enterprises in the south east Asian country of Vietnam following the country's WTO accession in 2007.

(with Robin Kaiji GONG, Yatang LIN, Jin WANG)

Belt & Road Impacts

State-Owned Enterprises in China's Belt and Road Initiative

We analyze the determining factors of Chinese foreign direct investment under the Belt and Road Initiative, with particular emphasis on the role of Chinese state-owned enterprises.

(with Albert Francis PARK, Angela TRITTO)

Import & Hate Crimes

The Impact of Import Competition and Trump's Election on Hate Crimes

I have studied how import competition and Trump's election have led to the rise in hate crimes in the United States.

Download the paper here.

Protests and Firm Performance
